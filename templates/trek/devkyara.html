DEVKYARA

DEV KYARA

Titled as the “TREK OF YEAR 2019”, Devkyara trek is a magnificent trekking expedition nestled between the most beautiful Rupin and Supin valleys of the Western Himalayas. Comparatively easier than the treks like Roopkund and Har-ki-doon, this trek finds itself in the bucket list of the true wayfarers. Right through the forests of pine and oak, the Devkyara valley opens to the enthralling scenes of the flower laden meadows and the pristine sightings of the majestic Rangnala, Banderpoonch, Kedarkantha and Swargarohini peaks. JAKHOL, the first base camp of the trek, is one amongst the last chain of villages within the Indo-China border. Fabulous arrangements have been made in the homestays, where the trekkers and the visitors are provided an atmosphere to dwell within the lifestyle of the local realm and enjoy the rich culture of the Himalayas. The best time to chase this expedition is from mid May to June and post monsoon from September to November.

THE BEAUTY OF DEVKYARA AS WELL AS THE PATH THAT LEADS TO THIS MESMERISING PLACE WILL DEFINITELY AMAZE YOU

SHORT ITINERARY of DEVKYARA TREK

DAY 1-Jakhol to Boincha (3.5 hrs by Cliffcat transport). Boincha (6765 ft) to Obra (7644 ft) - 3.7 km, 4 hrs Trek

DAY 2- Obra (7644 ft) to Bhaawa Waterfall (11,240 ft) - 8.15 km, (8 hrs trek)

DAY 3-Bhaawa Waterfall (11,240 ft) to Dev Kyara (13,451 ft) - 5 km, (6 hrs trek)

DAY 4-Expert brief , exploring DEVKYARA and back trek to Bhaawa Waterfall Campsite (11,240 ft) - 5 km, (5 hrs Trek).

DAY 5-Bhaawa Waterfall Campsite to Boincha - 12 km, 6 hrs Trek. Boincha to Jakhol – (3 hrs by Cliffcat transport)

DAY 6-Departure from Jakhol and arrive DEHRADUN,
